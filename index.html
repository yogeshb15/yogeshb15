What drives me? The pursuit of understanding the cosmos - from the largest structures in the universe to the smallest mathematical details that govern them. I believe mathematics is the language of the universe, and through it, we can uncover profound truths about our reality.

Research Focus

Matter Creation Cosmology

I'm deeply involved in exploring how the universe expands through gravitationally induced matter creation. This research investigates non-equilibrium thermodynamical processes within modified gravity theories, particularly f(Q) gravity. My work aims to understand how continuous matter creation might explain cosmic acceleration without requiring dark energy.

Modified Gravity Theories

My research in f(Q) gravity formalism examines alternative theories of gravity to explain cosmic acceleration and dark energy phenomena. I work with generalized Chaplygin gas models and perform statistical analysis using observational datasets to test the viability of these theoretical frameworks.

AI in Scientific Research

I combine my research background with AI development, creating mathematical AI models that can assist in complex scientific calculations and theoretical modeling. This interdisciplinary approach helps bridge traditional research with modern computational methods.

Late cosmic acceleration by matter creation cosmology in modified gravity

Annals of Physics, June 2025

Constraining Variable Generalized Chaplygin Gas model in Matter Creation Cosmology

Communication in Theoretical Physics, August 2024

Investigated the variable generalized Chaplygin gas model in matter creation cosmology, using Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods to constrain parameters with observational data including supernovae (Pantheon), cosmic chronometer (CC), BAO, DESI BAO, Quasars, and Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRB).

Matter creation cosmology with generalized Chaplygin gas

Astrophysics and Space Science, January 2024

Examined the dynamics of flat FLRW models powered by adiabatic matter creation with generalized Chaplygin gas equation of state, constraining the model using observational datasets including Type Ia supernovae Pantheon, H(z) and BAO.
